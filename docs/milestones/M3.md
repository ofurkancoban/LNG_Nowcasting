# M3: Terminal geofences and event detection

## Objective

Detect arrival, berth, and departure events for identified LNG carriers at
European regasification terminals using geofence polygons and dwell-time
rules.

## Deliverables

- `src/lng/events/geofence.py` (point-in-polygon checks, two-stage
  approach-zone + berth-polygon geofencing per docs/risks.md mitigation)
- `data/reference/terminal_geofences.geojson` (approach and berth polygons
  per European LNG terminal)
- `tests/test_geofence.py`
- `src/lng/events/detect.py` (arrival/berth/departure state machine with
  dwell-time threshold)
- `tests/test_event_detection.py`

## Acceptance criteria

- `pytest tests/test_geofence.py` passes with at least 8 cases, explicitly
  including: a point inside a berth polygon, a point inside the approach
  zone but outside the berth polygon, a point outside both, a point exactly
  on a polygon boundary, and at least one polygon crossing the antimeridian
  (longitude 180/-180) with a point correctly classified as inside despite
  the crossing.
- `pytest tests/test_event_detection.py` passes with cases covering: a
  vessel that arrives and dwells past the threshold (arrival confirmed), a
  vessel that enters the approach zone but leaves before the dwell
  threshold (no arrival recorded), and a vessel with a coverage gap during
  its dwell period (event detection tolerates the gap rather than resetting
  state incorrectly).
- Every terminal in `data/reference/terminal_geofences.geojson` validates as
  a legal GeoJSON polygon (checked via `shapely` load, no exceptions).

## Out of scope

- Cargo volume estimation from draught change (M5 nowcast concern, though
  draught data capture happens here if convenient).
- GIE ALSI ingestion (M4).

## Dependencies

M1 (raw AIS positions), M2 (LNG carrier identification, to scope which
vessels are evaluated against geofences).
