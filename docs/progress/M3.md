## Known caveats, carried forward honestly

- The Gate Rotterdam and Zeebrugge polygons are coarse illustrative
  rectangles around each terminal's approximate public coordinates, not
  surveyed berth boundaries. They are sufficient to prove the two-stage
  geofencing and dwell-detection pipeline but must be refined with accurate
  survey data before being used for real arrival detection.
- `TEST_ANTIMERIDIAN_FIXTURE` is a synthetic terminal invented purely to
  exercise the antimeridian-crossing test requirement; it is marked
  `is_test_fixture: true` in the GeoJSON properties and does not represent
  any real facility. No real European LNG terminal crosses the antimeridian.
- Only two terminals have real approximate polygons (Gate Rotterdam,
  Zeebrugge). Expanding to the full set of European LNG import terminals is
  not part of M3's acceptance criteria and is left as follow-up work.
